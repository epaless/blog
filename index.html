Otherworldly-Looking Deep-Sea Creatures

Why are we looking for aliens in space when they can be found deep in the ocean?

Here are some of the most uniquely looking creatures I found on the internet.

The Ribbon Sawtail Fish

Image from liveit.earth

The ribbon sawtail fish is a type of deep-sea fish that belongs to the family of barbeled dragonfish. It has a long, slender body with a distinctive saw-like extension on its tail. It is a bioluminescent fish, meaning it can produce light from special organs called photophores.
Any animal with long, transparent fangs and a light dangling from its chin will never feel like something "from Earth."

The Fangtooth

Image from newsweek.com

Fangtooths are beryciform fish of the family Anoplogastridae that live in the deep sea.
Fangtooth fish are physically horrifying, though they've never been a threat to humans. It's all in the face.

Frilled Shark

Image from hubpages.com

This shark (luckily) spends most of its time in dark waters far below the ocean surface. As a result, little is known about it... and maybe it's best we keep it that way.

Blobfish

Image from animalia-life.club

This unfortunate-looking fish was unanimously voted the "ugliest animal alive," which says a lot. Their strange bodies help them deal with the immense pressure of the bottom of the ocean, but nothing can save that face.
(This is how they look underwater.)

The Pelican Eel/Gulper Eel

When the gulper eel encounters prey, its mouth expands in an awesome way. This creates a giant net, compensating for its tiny eyes and lack of hunting talent.They also inflate their mouth to intimidate predators.

Sarcastic Fringehead

When this fish attacks, it looks like the Demogorgon from Stranger Things. This hot-tempered fish aggressively kisses anyone who goes near its territory.
