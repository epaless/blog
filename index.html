Otherworldly-Looking Deep-Sea Creatures

Why are we looking for aliens in space when they can be found deep in the ocean?

Here are some of the most uniquely looking creatures I found on the internet.

The Ribbon Sawtail Fish

Image from liveit.earth

The ribbon sawtail fish is a type of deep-sea fish that belongs to the family of barbeled dragonfish. It has a long, slender body with a distinctive saw-like extension on its tail. It is a bioluminescent fish, meaning it can produce light from special organs called photophores.
Any animal with long, transparent fangs and a light dangling from its chin will never feel like something "from Earth."

The Fangtooth

Image from newsweek.com

Fangtooths are beryciform fish of the family Anoplogastridae that live in the deep sea.
Fangtooth fish look horrifying, but they've never been a threat to humans given how small they actually is.

Fangtooth Facts!
  • Fangtooth fish are carnivores that will eat anything they can kill.
  • They have the largest teeth proportionate to their body size of any fish in the ocean.
  • They have extremely compressed bodies with huge heads, enormous jaws and a gruesome, corpse-like appearance.
Let's be thankful they're small

Frilled Shark

Image from hubpages.com

This shark fortunately spends most of its time deep in the ocean. As a result, little is known about it... and I think it's best we keep it that way.

Blobfish

Image from animalia-life.club

This unfortunate-looking fish was unanimously voted the "ugliest animal alive," which says a lot. Their strange bodies help them deal with the immense pressure of the bottom of the ocean, but nothing can save that face.
(This is how they look underwater.)

A fishy with a good personality

The Pelican Eel/Gulper Eel

Image from archaeology24.com

When the gulper eel encounters prey, its mouth expands like a balloon creating a giant net, compensating for its tiny eyes and lack of hunting talent.They also inflate their mouth to intimidate predators. They're a very odd creatures that gulp down things bigger than themselves and inside those enormous mouth is a series of teeth facing backwards ensuring that anything that enters its mouth can never get out.
It's a one way trip to hell, I guess?

Sarcastic Fringehead

Image from recentzone.com

The Sarcastic Fringehead is a fish native to the eastern Pacific and prefers to hide in objects and cavities, such as shells, burrows, and crevices. Female Sarcastic Fringeheads will look for a male with a desirable territory, she lays her eggs inside the male's home and then leaves. After which the male guards until they hatch.

When this fish opens its mouth, it looks like the Demogorgon from Stranger Things. This hot-tempered fish aggressively kisses anyone who goes near its territory.

If this is the Sarcastic Fringehead, I'd like to see the Serious Fringehead
